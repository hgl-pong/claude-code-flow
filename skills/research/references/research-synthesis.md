# Research Synthesis

Turn raw research data into actionable insights. Find patterns, quantify themes, connect evidence to recommendations.

## Accepted Inputs

- Interview transcripts
- Survey results (quantitative + open-ended)
- Usability test notes
- Support tickets
- NPS responses
- App store reviews

## Output Template

```markdown
## Executive Summary
[2-4 sentences: what was studied, key finding, top recommendation]

## Key Themes

### Theme 1: [Name]
- **Prevalence**: [X of Y participants / Z% of responses]
- **Summary**: [1-2 sentences]
- **Supporting Quotes**:
  > "Quote text here" — P1
  > "Another quote" — P3
- **Implication**: [What this means for the product]

## Insights → Opportunities
| Insight | Opportunity | Impact | Effort |
|---------|-------------|--------|--------|
| [Observed pattern] | [What to build/change] | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low |

## User Segments
| Segment | Characteristics | Needs | Est. Size |
|---------|----------------|-------|-----------|
| [Name] | [Behaviors/traits] | [What they need] | [Large/Medium/Small] |

## Recommendations
1. **[Priority: Must/Should/Could]** — [Recommendation]. Rationale: [Why].

## Questions for Further Research
- [What remains unknown after synthesis]

## Methodology Notes
- Participants: [N], Method: [type], Date: [range]
- Limitations: [biases, gaps, sample constraints]
```

## Synthesis Process

1. **Tag** — Label each data point with emergent codes
2. **Cluster** — Group codes into themes
3. **Quantify** — Count prevalence across participants
4. **Quote** — Select representative quotes per theme
5. **Connect** — Link themes to product opportunities
6. **Prioritize** — Rank recommendations by impact and effort

## Tips

- Include raw quotes with participant IDs — they ground recommendations in evidence.
- Separate observations from interpretations. "7 of 10 users missed the button" is observation. "The button is too subtle" is interpretation.
- Quantify where possible. "Most users" < "7 of 10 users" < "70% of users".
