# Design Knowledge Base

Reference these products for specific techniques — always state what you borrow AND what you deliberately break away from. Copy without divergence = template.

## Linear — Precision density, not minimalism
- What works: `#08090a` near-black surfaces, 4px grid discipline, hierarchy through weight not color, zero decorative elements
- What to steal: the density philosophy — every pixel earns its place
- What to avoid copying: Inter (too common), indigo accent (overused in dev tools)
- Best for: Developer tools, SaaS dashboards, data-heavy interfaces

## Vercel — Typographic confidence
- What works: monochrome palette forces typography to carry all meaning, uppercase micro-labels, generous whitespace as a statement
- What to steal: "nothing is decoration" discipline — if it's not content, remove it
- What to avoid copying: pure `#000`/`#fff` (cold), the exact letter-spacing values (theirs, not yours)
- Best for: Documentation, infrastructure, developer-facing products

## Stripe — Editorial asymmetry
- What works: asymmetric hero compositions breaking the centered layout default, light font weights for display text, editorial section rhythm
- What to steal: sections that feel visually distinct from each other, not one repeating pattern
- What to avoid copying: `#635bff` purple (now everyone's "premium" color), gradient hero backgrounds
- Best for: Trust-critical interfaces, payment flows, landing pages

## Notion — Content-first spacing
- What works: warm off-white surfaces (`#f7f6f3` not pure white), serif + sans pairing where the font mix IS the brand
- What to steal: the idea that layout should feel like a physical medium (paper, notebook)
- What to avoid copying: their exact neutral palette (looks like Notion), the loose density (only fits content tools)
- Best for: Knowledge tools, writing interfaces, educational products

## Apple — Extreme scale contrast
- What works: massive display type paired with tiny captions — the scale ratio IS the design, full-bleed photography as primary color
- What to steal: treating scale difference as the main design tool (not color, not gradients)
- What to avoid copying: centered composition (overused), SF Pro (system font, no differentiation)
- Best for: Consumer showcase, premium product pages, high-stakes first impressions

## Craft Docs — Warmth through imperfection
- What works: slightly rounded but not bubbly, warm grays with red undertones, micro-animations with spring physics
- What to steal: the "handmade but precise" tension — careful layouts that still feel touched by a human
- What to avoid copying: their specific off-white (context-dependent)
- Best for: Productivity tools, creative apps, consumer-leaning SaaS

## Loom — Personality in empty states
- What works: empty states that tell a story, loading states with brand voice, onboarding that celebrates the user
- What to steal: writing microcopy before designing the surrounding layout
- What to avoid copying: the video-centric color system (product-specific)
- Best for: Any app where user activation matters, onboarding flows

## Figma — Layered density with confident color
- What works: bold accent color with purpose (`#7B61FF` only on interactive, not decorative), toolbars that pack information without feeling cramped, contextual panels that adapt to selection
- What to steal: the idea that density and clarity are not opposites
- What to avoid copying: their exact purple (theirs, not yours), the canvas-centric layout (product-specific)
- Best for: Creative tools, canvas interfaces, complex SaaS with many states

## Raycast — Speed as a design principle
- What works: keyboard-first visual language, monospace + geometric sans pairing, color as semantic signal not decoration, dark-first palette with precise opacity
- What to steal: designing for the power user without alienating the new user
- What to avoid copying: their specific extension architecture (product-specific)
- Best for: Productivity tools, command interfaces, developer utilities

## Are.na — Deliberate anti-polish
- What works: grid density that respects content over decoration, restraint as an aesthetic choice, typography that steps back and lets the content speak
- What to steal: the courage to look "undesigned" because the content IS the design
- What to avoid copying: the minimal chrome (requires strong content to support it)
- Best for: Content curation, creative tools, portfolio/showcase interfaces
